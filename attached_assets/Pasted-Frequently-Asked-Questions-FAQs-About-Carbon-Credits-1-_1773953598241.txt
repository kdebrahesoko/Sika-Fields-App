Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
🌱 About Carbon Credits
1. What are carbon credits?

Carbon credits represent the removal or reduction of one metric ton of CO₂ (or equivalent greenhouse gases) from the atmosphere. Companies and individuals purchase them to offset their emissions and reduce their environmental impact.

2. How do carbon credits work?

Carbon credits are generated by projects that remove or reduce emissions (e.g., planting trees).
At SikaFields:

Farmers implement sustainable practices

Carbon impact is measured and verified

Credits are issued

Buyers purchase credits to offset emissions

Credits are retired permanently

3. How are carbon credits generated and verified?

Credits are generated through activities like reforestation or regenerative farming.
SikaFields ensures:

Accurate measurement using scientific models

Monitoring and reporting (MRV)

Third-party verification (where applicable)

This ensures credits are:

Real

Measurable

Permanent

Additional

4. What types of projects generate carbon credits on SikaFields?

SikaFields focuses on nature-based solutions, including:

Agroforestry

Reforestation and afforestation

Regenerative agriculture

Soil carbon improvement

Energy-efficient cookstoves

5. Why are carbon credits important?

They:

Help reduce global emissions

Support sustainable farming

Provide income to rural communities

Enable companies to meet climate goals

🌾 For Farmers & Landowners
6. How do I join SikaFields as a farmer?

You can:

Register through the mobile app

Work with a local field agent

You’ll provide details about your land and farming practices.

7. What are the requirements to join?

To participate, farmers must:

Be within the project zone

Be willing to participate fully

Own or have rights to farmland (with witnesses if needed)

Have minimum 1 hectare (2.5 acres)

Be willing to plant or maintain trees (e.g., mahogany, neem, cassia)

Ensure existing tree crops are not older than 5 years (new farms encouraged)

Follow environmental regulations

Participate in training programs

Support inclusivity (youth, vulnerable groups encouraged)

Commit to maintaining trees for at least 30 years

Adopt sustainable practices (e.g., minimal tillage, low pesticide use)

👉 This is strict by design—carbon markets depend on long-term credibility, not short-term participation.

8. Do I still own my land?

Yes. You retain full ownership and control of your land at all times.

9. How do I earn money?

You earn when:

Your project is verified

Carbon credits are issued

Credits are sold to buyers

10. How and when do I get paid?

Payments are made after credits are sold via:

Mobile money (e.g., MTN MoMo)

Bank transfer

Local payment systems

⚠️ Important: Payments are not immediate—carbon verification takes time.

11. Do I need internet access?

No. The app supports offline data collection, and you can sync when connected.

🏢 For Buyers (Companies & Individuals)
12. How can I purchase carbon credits?

You can:

Browse verified projects

Select credits

Pay securely through the platform

13. What determines the price of carbon credits?

Pricing depends on:

Project type (e.g., forestry vs energy)

Market demand

14. What happens after I purchase credits?

Credits are retired permanently

You receive a certificate of offset

Your impact is recorded in your dashboard

15. Can I track my environmental impact?

Yes. You’ll see:

Total CO₂ offset

Project details and location

Community impact (e.g., farmer income, trees planted)
