 Overview SikaFields Technology, headquartered in Dubai with active operations in Accra, develops carbon credits by aggregating smallholder farmland into registry ready project portfolios. The company is a spinout of Esoko, leveraging 17 years of field infrastructure and direct access to 2 million verified smallholder farmers. This foundation enables SikaFields to manage the full credit lifecycle while operating the monitoring, reporting and verification (MRV) systems that meet independent registry standards. This operating model defines how value reaches smallholder farmers. Farmers receive 60% of carbon revenue generated by their plots. Of that share, 32% is disbursed as direct mobile payments and 28% is delivered as in-kind benefits, including seedlings, agronomic services, training and MRV support. Seedlings and agronomic services are procured and distributed through DigiMakt and are recorded in the MRV system so that in-kind benefits are auditable at the plot level. Esoko supplies the digital backbone required to operate MRV at smallholder scale. The Insyt platform supports biometric identity verification, GPS plot mapping, mobile field logging and satellite aligned data capture. Insyt also integrates mobile wallets for secure payments and connects farmers to input sellers and commodity buyers through DigiMakt. These systems ensure unique farmer identification, validated plot geometry and an auditable chain linking field activity, payments and project records. SikaFields manages agronomy, field execution and registry alignment. Field teams deliver seedlings procured via DigiMakt and regional nursery partners, train farmers in agroforestry mosaics and carbon farming practices, and maintain continuous field observations. The MRV system integrates mobile reporting, remote sensing and structured validator checks so that each plot produces a continuous, audit ready dataset from enrollment through monitoring. Seedling distribution and agronomic interventions are logged in Insyt and accounted for as part of the 28% in-kind benefits. Verification follows two registry tracks. Plots that meet Open Forest Protocol (OFP) criteria undergo two 6 month monitoring and reporting cycles followed by independent validator review and issuance on the OFP Atlas. Plots that do not meet OFP parameters follow the Verra VM0047 methodology. For biologically mature or otherwise eligible plots that enter live MRV workflows, the platform targets issuance within roughly 12 to 18 months; newly planted plots follow longer biological timelines. At the company level SikaFields targets OFP issuance in Q4 2026 and Verra issuance in Q4 2027. Together, Esoko’s digital systems and SikaFields’ field operations produce continuous, auditable plot level datasets and transparent benefit distribution that support independent validation and registry issuance.  Market Opportunity Global demand for high integrity carbon removals is increasing as major economies tighten decarbonization commitments. Remaining within a 1.5°C pathway requires a 43% reduction in global emissions by 2030. The European Union has adopted a 90% emissions reduction target for 2040 and plans to accept credits from developing countries for compliance beginning in 2036. Across the Gulf region more than $100 billion has been committed to national net zero plans, and corporate procurement is shifting toward verified removal credits as regulators and buyers treat removals as the credible instrument for balancing residual emissions.
